Market Expansion Readiness Review

A concise decision memo on launch readiness, operating risks, and the near-term investment required to protect adoption quality.

Recommendation Go

Proceed with a two-city rollout while holding enterprise automation for phase two.

Prepared for Leadership Team 15 June 2026
What matters

Executive Summary

Demand is validated

Qualified pipeline is 1.8x the launch threshold, with the strongest signal from mid-market operations teams.

Service quality is the constraint

Launch risk is concentrated in onboarding capacity, not acquisition or product completeness.

Scope control is decisive

Deferring advanced automation preserves delivery reliability and reduces support load during the first 60 days.

Decision requested

Approve the phased rollout plan and allocate two implementation specialists through the end of Q3.

Recommended Path

Launch in Shanghai and Singapore with standard onboarding, weekly executive telemetry, and a narrow success definition: activated accounts, time to value, and first-month retention.

  • Keep launch messaging focused on workflow reliability and measurable handoff speed.
  • Route complex integration requests into a controlled design partner queue.
  • Review expansion readiness after 30 activated customers or 45 days, whichever comes first.
Operating view

Risks and Mitigations

Onboarding queue pressure

Mitigation: cap weekly new starts and prioritize customers with complete implementation inputs.

Enterprise feature pull

Mitigation: publish a phase-two roadmap and require approval for any custom launch commitments.
